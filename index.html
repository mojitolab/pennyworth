SEIS Scheme Incentives

Service Exports from India Scheme (SEIS) is aimed at promoting and maximizing export from India by providing incentives for eligible exporters. Introduced under the Foreign Trade Policy (FTP) - 2015-20, the Government of India, SEIS replaced the previous scheme Served from India Scheme (SFIS) under Foreign Trade Policy, 2009-15.

The SEIS expanded the scope of services for which the benefits were available under the SFIS scheme. The SEIS incentives can be availed by all the companies in India which are exporting services. SFIS incentives were not available to SEZ units, SEIS incentives are offered to SEZ units as well.

Under SEIS, the exporters of notified services are incentivized not in the form of cash remittance but in the form of Duty Credit Scrip. Depending on the nature of service, Duty Credit Scrip is calculated as specific parentage in the range of 3% to 5% of Net Foreign Earnings, and covers service providers located in India. SEIS scrips are transferrable, can also be sold to other assessee and can be used for payment of various taxes levied on goods and services.

Eligibility criteria to claim benefits under the SEIS
To be eligible for duty credit scrips, Service providers of notified services should be located in India and should have minimum net free foreign exchange earnings of US$15,000 in the year of rendering the services. For individual service providers and sole proprietorship, such minimum net free foreign exchange earnings criteria is US$10,000 in the preceding financial year is required to be eligible for the scheme. Further, the exporter should have to have an active Import Export Code (IE Code) at the time of rendering such services for which rewards are claimed in order to claim reward under the SEIS scheme.

Service NOT eligible to claim benefits under the SEIS
Foreign exchange received for other purposes like equity, debt, donation, loan repayment, etc. are excluded.

Preparation of Financials

Preparation of financial statements is one of the most crucial steps in accounting. Financial statements are the end product of the accounting system in any firm and they are tools for providing useful information to external users. The preparation includes income statement, statement of retained earnings, balance sheet and cash flow statement.

Income statement: Contains details on revenues, expenses, and the resulting net income. It depicts the overall profitability of a business for the time period in question. Also known as a profit and loss statement, income statement provides details the expenses for the time period and losses from any activity.

Statement of retained earnings: Covers the retained earnings at the beginning and end of the accounting period. Information required for preparing these reports include beginning retained earnings, obtained from the previous statement of retained earnings; net income, obtained from the income statement; and dividends paid during the accounting period.

Balance sheet: Offers details of the assets, liabilities, and shareholder equity of the company. It is prepared using balances of all asset accounts; balances of all liability accounts; capital stock balance; and retained earnings.

Cash flow statement: Explains the reasons for changes in the cash balance, and offers details about sources and uses of cash in the operating, financing, and investing activities of the enterprise.
